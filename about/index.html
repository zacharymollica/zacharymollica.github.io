Working full time at AA's Hooke Park campus (UK), Zachary Mollica is a Canadian architect and maker whose work focuses on the integration of digital methods alongside traditional craft knowledge and natural materials.

BACKSTORY

My father and grandfather, both carpenters, involved me in their work from an early age. Before architecture school I studied liberal arts and physics, and after graduating worked for design practices in Amsterdam, Lunenburg, Toronto and Vancouver.

Then I landed in Hooke Park. Embedded in a site that merges camping, hi-tech workshop and design studio, the five years since have supplied the opportunity to develop a specialist skillset – engaging with my surrounds in a wholly new way.

Variety of experience is my strongest trait – I like to work on a bit of everything.
