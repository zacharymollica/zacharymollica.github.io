About Zac

Variety of experience is my strongest trait - I like to work on a bit of everything.

Growing up I worked with my father and grandfather, both carpenters. I studied liberal arts at the University of King's College and physics and sustainability at Dalhousie University before entering the school of architecture. Since, I have worked for a number of architecture and design practices in Amsterdam (NL), Vancouver (BC), Toronto (ON) and Lunenburg (NS).

For the past 16 months I have been in Hooke Park, the Architectural Association School of Architecture's forest campus. Supported by a diverse faculty, the MArch Design & Make program presents a unique opportunity to develop innovative building techniques through the combination of advanced technologies, craft knowledge and natural materials. The focus of my time in Hooke has been the realization of the Wood Chip Barn - a robotically fabricated structure composed of tree forks which spans 25m.
