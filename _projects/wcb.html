AA Design & Make studio project. A robotically fabricated structure made up of forks from beech trees. Spanning 25m x 10m and rising to a height of 8m, the truss is intended to contain 400 cubic meters of wood chip which will be used to heat the Hooke Park campus. Integrating Grasshopper, 3D scanning and a KUKA 6 axis robotic arm with manual techniques, the project focussed on developing the necessary workflows for utilizing complex pieces of timber generally disregarded in contemporary timber construction.

An initial survey of Hooke's beech compartments identified 204 potential forks in standing trees, documenting them photographically. Using an analysis script developed in Grasshopper a shortlist of forks was created - looking for suitable angles and diameters. With this list, we worked alongside our forester to fell and extract 25 forks. Back in the yard, a digital mesh was generated by 3D scanning each fork. After extracting centrelines from each of these, another Grasshopper script was used to optimize the placement of these forks within an overall truss volume that had been drawn in collaboration with ARUP engineers, selecting the best fitting 20. A wireframe model of the complete truss output from this script was used to generate the fabrication instructions for the robotic arm's tool paths. With all of the individual forks milled they were assembled in two halves in the Big Shed before being transported to site for the final assembly.
