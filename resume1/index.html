Academic

Warden, Hooke Park (2019–)

AA School of Architecture – London + Hooke Park

Responsible for managing Hooke Park’s academic use, finances, operations, infrastructure and health and safety. As Warden I work closely with the AA school director and senior management, the Hooke Park staff, and tutors and departments from across the school to develop Hooke Park as a unique academic facility, public resource, and leading site of architectural research.



Specialist Lecturer, Design + Make (2016–)

AA School of Architecture – London + Hooke Park

Specialist Lecturer for the AA’s MArch/MSc Design + Make programme. Working closely with the programme’s directors and students, I am responsible for delivering an integrated manner of teaching - providing studio tutoring and instruction in digital tools such as 3D scanning and a range of fabrication strategies – in order to enable the realisation of ambitious architectural constructs.



SummerBuild Programme Head (2016–18)

AA Visiting School – Hooke Park

Responsible for organising and directing an international team of students and practitioners from a range of fields who join students of the Design + Make programme to help construct active projects in Hooke Park.



Robotic Fabrications Studio Tutor (2016–17)

AA Visiting School – Hooke Park

Tutor for the AA’s Robotic Fabrications visiting school with director Emmanuel Vercruysse. Re-inventing traditional making techniques and tools, we apply them alongside digital fabrication tools and 3D scanning to develop innovative processes for physical production.



MakeLab Technical Tutor (2016–17)

AA Visiting School – Hooke Park, UK

Tutor for the AA’s Makelab Visiting School along with director Jeroen van Ameijde. A yearly five-day workshop engaging with computer-aided architectural design and fabrication aimed at developing new modes of design conception. A diverse range of software is employed.



Workshop Assistant (2011–12)

Dalhousie School of Architecture – Halifax

While studying at Dalhousie School of Architecture, prior carpentry experience enabled me to work as a teaching assistant in the school’s workshop - supervising my fellow students’ use of machinery.
